## 1. The Name and What It Means

**Meridian** is the brand for this ecosystem. A meridian is the reference line from which all positions on Earth are measured — every coordinate on the planet is expressed relative to it. That is precisely the role this system plays: **the universal reference layer against which all value systems — fiat, crypto, and the new contribution credits — are measured, reconciled, and made interoperable.**

The full system name is **Meridian: The Universal Value Ledger (UVL)**. Internally the protocol is **MVP — the Meridian Value Protocol**. I'll use *Meridian* throughout.

It is not a new currency competing with the others. It is the layer *above* all macroeconomies — the one that, as you put it, **includes, develops, adjusts, fixes, and filters all known macroeconomies**, while introducing a new asset class the old systems structurally cannot represent: **value minted from verified human flourishing** (the 2030B credits, and the open-ended family that follows them).

## 2. The Three Currency Families

Meridian unifies three families behind one ledger and one API. This is the core architectural insight: they are *not* three products, but three issuance models over one double-entry ledger.

| Family | Examples | What backs it | Issuance model |
|--------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Reserve-backed** | USD, EUR, TND, gold tokens, CBDCs | Custodied real-world assets (1:1) | Mint-on-deposit, burn-on-redemption |
| **Distributed / cryptographic** | BTC, ETH, project tokens | Cryptographic scarcity / consensus | External chains, bridged into Meridian |
| **Contribution credits (new)** | CTC, TIC, VTC, INC, SCC, WPC, WDC, JEC, FLC, GRC, *…∞* | Verified human action | **Proof-of-Verified-Contribution (PoVC)** |

The third family is the genuinely new invention. A **TIC** (Trust & Integrity Credit) only comes into existence when a human performs a verified honest act; a **VTC** (Vitality Credit) when verified health behavior occurs. These are not earned by buying, mining, or staking — they are **minted by living well, attested by independent verifiers.** Because the list is open-ended ("many will be added"), the architecture treats *currency type* as a first-class, pluggable, governed entity — not a hardcoded enum.

## 4. Problems with the Old Ecosystems (and how Meridian addresses each)

Because you asked specifically to document old-ecosystem failures and new-ecosystem risk scenarios so they can be solved in the security milestone, here is the full accounting. This is the foundation of the security work, written in prose so it reads as a threat-and-remedy register rather than a checklist.

**Failures of fiat macroeconomies.** Centralized fiat suffers from opaque reserves (you cannot independently verify a central bank or stablecoin issuer holds what it claims), inflation and debasement driven by unaccountable issuance, transaction censorship and financial exclusion, settlement latency across borders, and the structural inability to represent non-monetary value (health, honesty, ecological contribution) at all. Meridian answers these with cryptographically attested reserve proofs (every reserve-backed mint is provable against custodied assets via published attestations), a governed and transparent issuance policy logged to an append-only transparency log, censorship-resistance through the distributed family, and ISO 20022 native messaging for low-friction cross-border settlement.

**Failures of crypto macroeconomies.** Distributed systems brought their own pathologies: 51% / consensus attacks, double-spending, smart-contract exploits and reentrancy bugs, private-key loss and theft, wash trading and market manipulation, rug pulls and exit scams, MEV and front-running, extreme volatility unsuited to everyday value transfer, energy waste in proof-of-work, and pervasive regulatory non-compliance (AML/KYC gaps). Meridian treats external chains as *bridged* sources behind hardened, audited bridge adapters with rate limits and circuit breakers, never trusting a bridge as a source of truth without confirmation depth and reconciliation; it keeps the authoritative balance in its own audited double-entry ledger; and it layers compliance (travel-rule, sanctions screening) at the protocol edge rather than bolting it on.

**The genuinely new risks introduced by contribution credits.** This is where the security milestone must concentrate, because no prior system has minted money from human behavior. The dominant threats are: **fake-action fraud** (claiming a TIC for an honest act never performed); **Sybil attacks** (one human operating thousands of identities to farm credits — catastrophic at 8B-user scale); **oracle/verifier compromise** (corrupting the attester that mints credits); **collusion** between claimant and verifier; **replay** (submitting the same verified action twice for double minting); **gamification drift** (people optimizing the measurable proxy instead of the real virtue — the source documents repeatedly forbid gamifying these credits); **biometric/data-privacy harm** (verifying health or honesty requires sensitive data that must never become a surveillance tool); and **coercion** (credits earned under duress, e.g., an employer forcing credit-earning behavior). 

The remedies, which become the security milestone's backbone: **Proof-of-Personhood** for strong, privacy-preserving one-human-one-identity guarantees against Sybil; **multi-attester quorum minting** so no single verifier can mint (M-of-N independent oracles must agree); **nonce-bound, time-bound attestations** to defeat replay; **anti-collusion rotation and random verifier assignment**; **zero-knowledge attestations** so a credit can be proven valid without exposing the underlying sensitive data (you prove "this person met the vitality criterion" without revealing biometrics); **rate-limited, decaying issuance** with policy-engine circuit breakers to neutralize farming and gamification at the macro level; and **non-transferability or constrained transferability** for credits that should reward being rather than trading — directly honoring the source documents' insistence that these credits "are not gamified" and reward "documented disengagement, not louder engagement."

Every one of these maps to a concrete deliverable in the prompt below.

## 7. Where to Go Next

I'd suggest the natural next steps are, in order: nail down the **Proof-of-Personhood** approach (this is the hardest single problem at 8B scale and gates the whole contribution-credit family); define the **first concrete contribution-credit spec** end-to-end (pick TIC or VTC and specify exactly what action, what verifier, what ZK proof, what issuance rate) as a reference implementation the other ∞ credits will follow; and decide the **governance model** for who can register new currency types and change policy (the license assigns this to a Steward/committee — that body needs definition).

A few honest caveats worth stating plainly: a currency minted from *verified human behavior* is powerful precisely because it can shade into a social-credit system, which is why I made the anti-punishment floor a hard license condition rather than a guideline — that line is the ethical spine of the whole project and deserves the most scrutiny. And at genuine planetary scale, the proof-of-personhood and privacy layers are research-grade problems, not solved engineering; the architecture above is sound, but those two components warrant dedicated design review before any launch.
