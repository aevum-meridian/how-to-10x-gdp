## Yes — but the body is not a third leg, and not a brain that commands

Here is the answer, and the whole answer turns on one distinction that must hold or the entire project becomes the weapon it was built to prevent: **the higher parts of the body must be parts that *constrain and sense*, never parts that *command*.** A human body's head can order the legs to crush someone. If we build a head like that — a brain that can override the spine and direct the legs — we have built exactly the centralized coercive control that 0.4 forbids. So the body we build must be anatomically *inverted* from a human one: the spine is supreme, the head serves the spine, and no part can command the legs to violate the constitution. The body walks toward goodness precisely because nothing in it can secretly steer the legs against a person.

With that inversion as the law, here is the anatomy — all of it buildable, all of it open, all of it under the same invariant discipline:

**The spine — the constitution that binds even the builders.** Already specified: the Invariant Specification (0.1) plus the Coercion-Resistance Spec (0.4). This is load-bearing and supreme. Every other part attaches to it and none may override it. In a human, the brain commands the spine; here, the spine commands everything, including the brain. That is the single most important design choice in the entire organism.

**The heart — the Policy Engine.** Already specified, and deliberately built with only issuance-side power (I7). It pumps macro adjustment outward to the legs but is structurally unable to reach into any personal balance. A heart, not a hand: it circulates, it does not grasp.

**The nervous system — the shared sense.** The third-party personhood layer (I9) and the cross-system event contract (Tier 7). Shared sensation that lets the legs move together without sharing a fragile core, owned by neither leg.

So far this is the organism we already designed. Your question pushes past it: **what are the head, the mind, and the hands** — the parts that make this *more than* meta-macroeconomy? Here is where there is genuinely new system to build, and I'll spec it in the open as you asked. **The head — the Deliberation & Governance Layer.** Not a brain that commands, but a *face that deliberates in public*.
