# Aevum — Trade‑off Register (v3)

> **This is a versioned, governance‑controlled artifact, not marketing copy.**
> It states, for each contested axis of money, exactly where Aevum's current
> parameters sit and why. You cannot quietly change a trade‑off: amending this
> register is a governance act with a paper trail.
>
> The machine‑readable counterpart is `App\Support\TradeOffRegister`, surfaced
> via `GET /api/v1/trade-off-register`. This document and that class must agree.

**Register version:** `3.0.0`

---

## How to read this

Each entry names an **axis** (two competing goods), the **position** Aevum
currently takes on that axis, the **reason**, and the **reversal path** (how the
position could legitimately be changed).

---

### 1. Privacy ⟷ Compliance

- **Position:** Privacy‑preserving compliance by default. Prove "not sanctioned"
  or "over 18" via selective‑disclosure ZK rather than revealing identity.
  Regulated tunnels enforce travel‑rule/AML where legally required.
- **Reason:** Both are real goods; Aevum refuses to make users choose between
  dignity and lawful access. Child‑safety protections **fail closed**.
- **Reversal path:** Constitutional process; the child‑safety floor is a
  `ConstitutionalParameter` requiring near‑unanimity.

### 2. Autonomy ⟷ Stability

- **Position:** "Money that proposes, humans that decide." Only **Tier‑0**
  formally‑verified, capped automatic rules act without a human; AI is advisory.
- **Reason:** Unbounded "bounds" are where the danger lives. Liability attaches
  to the ratifying body (Tier‑1) or the audited spec (Tier‑0).
- **Reversal path:** Expanding Tier‑0 scope requires formal verification + a
  constitutional vote.

### 3. Inclusion ⟷ Sybil‑resistance

- **Position:** A four‑rung identity ladder. Rung‑0 is open to everyone; full
  `$UNA` and 1‑human‑1‑vote require Rung‑3 personhood. The Rung‑1 probationary
  pool has a **hard constitutional issuance cap**.
- **Reason:** Personhood at 8B does not exist. `$UNA`'s reach equals the reach of
  the best personhood providers, and the hardest‑to‑verify people most need UBI.
  We bound the loss instead of pretending the problem is solved.
- **Reversal path:** Raising the Rung‑1 cap is a constitutional act.

### 4. Speed/cost ⟷ Spam‑resistance & finality

- **Position:** **Not feeless.** Fees are tiny on L2 and **sponsored/abstracted**
  (protocol sponsors `$UNA` recipients, merchants sponsor payments). A small
  irreducible cost always exists underneath.
- **Reason:** Feeless systems get spammed; that is why durable ones charge
  something. Speed/low cost is bought with explicit L2 trust assumptions.
- **Reversal path:** Fee‑model parameters are governance‑controlled.

### 5. Censorship‑resistance ⟷ Ethical filtering

- **Position:** **User‑sovereign filtering by default.** The protocol provides
  multi‑sourced, contestable labels; each wallet sets its own rules. A
  *global* block is reserved for legally‑compelled cases and requires the full
  constitutional process (high quorum, time delay, written justification,
  appeal path).
- **Reason:** A system that can blacklist a national currency exercises a
  foreign‑policy power; Aevum makes that power expensive and impossible to
  invoke secretly.
- **Reversal path:** Global blocks are timelocked and appealable by design.

### 6. Personhood accuracy ⟷ Fairness/accountability

- **Position:** The aggregated personhood score is a **consequential algorithmic
  decision**: published & versioned aggregation, explainable denials, mandatory
  human appeal, independent fairness audits across demographics/regions.
- **Reason:** Denying `$UNA` on a probabilistic score is financial exclusion and
  will attract algorithmic‑accountability regulation. We treat it as such.
- **Reversal path:** Aggregation changes are register amendments + audits.

## Currency scope statements (on the record)

- **`$FLUX` is a community‑circulation experiment**, not a store of value and not
  a mass‑market currency. Expected: high velocity, low savings demand, small
  opt‑in user base. Holding `$FLUX` is *supposed* to be a losing trade vs.
  `$AEV`/`$PEG`. Marketing may **not** describe it as general‑purpose.
- **`$UNA`'s first stabiliser is an on‑chain velocity measure**, not a global
  purchasing‑power index (which is harder than a national CPI). A price index is
  earned later, with published methodology and governance over composition.
- **`$PEG` (regulated) and `$PEG+` (experimental) are distinct products** with
  distinct tickers. `$PEG+` is jurisdiction‑gated, requires risk acknowledgment,
  and is **never** marketed as a stablecoin.
- **`$MAKE` replaces the retired EEG `$FOCUS`.** It rewards verifiable output,
  not measured cognition, and carries no biometric data.

## Child‑safety floor (non‑negotiable)

No Aevum feature ever profiles, measures, sexualises, or financially
incentivises the bodies, attention, or appearance of minors. Personhood and age
systems **fail closed** for under‑18 protection. This is a
`ConstitutionalParameter` requiring near‑unanimity to alter.

## `$AEV` value‑accrual (anti‑plutocracy)

A portion of protocol fees (swaps, currency minting, tunnel usage) flows to a
**public‑goods fund**. `$AEV` staking grants **fee discounts**, not direct
revenue — aligning incentives without reinforcing whale dominance.
